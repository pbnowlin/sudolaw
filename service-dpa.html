Data Protection
Addenda & Privacy

Global data protection law is complex and jurisdiction-specific. SUDOLAW drafts, reviews, and negotiates data protection agreements that meet current requirements across the jurisdictions that matter to your business.

Overview

Data protection obligations follow data — not borders. An organization that collects personal data from EU residents, California residents, or users in dozens of other jurisdictions faces a layered web of legal requirements that govern how that data is processed, transferred, and protected. SUDOLAW advises on those obligations and handles the contracts and documentation that give effect to them.

What We Do

  • Data Processing Agreement (DPA) drafting and negotiation
  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) — EU and UK variants
  • Transfer Impact Assessments (TIA) and Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA)
  • Privacy policy and cookie notice drafting and review
  • Vendor data processing due diligence
  • Data subject rights response frameworks and procedures
  • Breach notification legal analysis and drafting
  • Records of Processing Activities (RoPA) development
  • Cross-border data transfer mechanism selection and implementation

Vendor Data Processing

Most data protection frameworks require organizations to have contracts in place with vendors who process personal data on their behalf. SUDOLAW reviews vendor-proposed DPAs, negotiates terms that align with your legal obligations and risk tolerance, and ensures the resulting agreements are legally sufficient under applicable law.

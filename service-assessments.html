Cybersecurity
Assessments

Security assessments generate sensitive findings. SUDOLAW provides legal counsel throughout the assessment process — from engagement structuring through remediation guidance and regulatory reporting.

Overview

Security assessments — gap analyses, penetration tests, vendor audits, and compliance reviews — are among the most legally sensitive documents an organization produces. The findings they contain can inform litigation, trigger regulatory disclosure obligations, and expose leadership to liability if mishandled. SUDOLAW advises on how to structure and manage assessment engagements to reduce legal exposure and navigate downstream obligations.

What We Do

  • Assessment engagement structuring and legal review
  • NYDFS 23 NYCRR Part 500 compliance assessments and annual certification support
  • SOC 2 and ISO 27001 gap analysis legal review
  • PCI-DSS scoping and legal counsel for QSA engagements
  • Multi-jurisdictional cybersecurity controls audits
  • Findings documentation and remediation prioritization
  • Board-level and CISO reporting on legal exposure and risk
  • Regulatory disclosure analysis following assessment findings

Remediation Guidance

After an assessment, organizations face decisions about how to prioritize remediation, what to document, and what regulatory obligations may have been triggered by findings. SUDOLAW advises on the legal dimensions of those decisions — helping organizations address findings in a way that reduces ongoing exposure.

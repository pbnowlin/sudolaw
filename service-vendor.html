Vendor & Contract
Negotiation

Technology contracts are security documents. SUDOLAW reviews and negotiates vendor agreements with an InfoSec-first lens, ensuring security obligations are enforceable and liability terms reflect actual breach exposure.

Overview

Most organizations rely on dozens or hundreds of third-party vendors who touch sensitive data, critical systems, or regulated environments. The contracts governing those relationships determine whether the organization has meaningful recourse when a vendor breach occurs — or whether it is left exposed by liability caps, indemnification carve-outs, and security obligation language that was never intended to be enforced.

SUDOLAW reviews and negotiates technology contracts with the perspective of someone who understands what a real security failure costs — not just what standard contract language says.

What We Do

  • SaaS, cloud, and managed service agreement review and negotiation
  • Liability cap negotiation with breach cost and regulatory exposure analysis
  • Security Exhibit and Information Security Addendum drafting
  • Business Associate Agreement (BAA) negotiation under HIPAA
  • Third-party risk management legal frameworks
  • Software escrow and source code access agreements
  • Cyber insurance policy review and coverage gap analysis
  • Vendor security assessment legal support
  • Incident notification and cooperation clause negotiation

Liability Cap Negotiation

Standard SaaS and cloud contracts typically cap vendor liability at a multiple of fees paid — often a figure that is a fraction of what a breach at that vendor would actually cost the customer. SUDOLAW advises on how to analyze those caps in light of realistic breach scenarios and negotiates terms that provide meaningful protection where it matters most.

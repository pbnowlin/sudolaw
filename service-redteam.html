Red Team Legal
Authorization

Offensive security operations require more than technical planning — they require a legally sound authorization framework that protects the organization, the operator, and the engagement itself.

Overview

Red team engagements, penetration tests, and assumed breach exercises carry real legal exposure under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act, state computer fraud statutes, and — for engagements touching third-party infrastructure — potentially international law. A well-drafted authorization framework eliminates ambiguity about what is and is not in scope, who has authorized the activity, and what remedies are available if something goes wrong.

SUDOLAW drafts and negotiates the legal documents that make offensive security operations defensible — protecting both the organization commissioning the work and the operators executing it.

What We Do

  • Rules of engagement (ROE) drafting and legal review
  • Scope authorization memos and written authorization documentation
  • CFAA and state computer fraud law compliance analysis
  • Third-party and cloud infrastructure authorization frameworks
  • Operator indemnification and liability agreements
  • Purple team and assumed breach engagement authorization
  • Physical intrusion and social engineering legal parameters
  • Post-engagement findings handling and documentation guidance

Third-Party Authorization

Engagements that touch cloud providers (AWS, Azure, GCP), SaaS platforms, or other third-party infrastructure require careful handling. Most major cloud providers have their own penetration testing policies and require advance notice or approval for certain activities. SUDOLAW advises on these requirements and ensures the authorization framework accounts for all relevant third-party obligations.
